    • What is Solar System & Why we need it?

    Solar System is basically a renewable energy source. It is energy converter from one medium to other, that is also useful for our needs. Solar System is a direct sunlight source, which converts the high-density light into useful electrical energy. In our household, we see number of different home Appliances. They all work on the basis of electrical energy. They Required energy to work properly and fulfill our needs in daily-life.

    How Pakistan Beneficiary for Solar System?

    In Pakistan, there is a suitable environment for Solar System, that gives a reasonable energy in daily-life. Although, there are some variation in efficiency of the system. The system efficiency (Energy Production Rate) drops down due to some reasons like; Increase in environment Temperature, System Wiring losses, System derating & Dust Accumulate on the System.

    Types of Solar System Installed

    There are mainly three different types of solar system installed:

    1. On-Grid
    2. Off-Grid
    3. Hybrid

    On-Grid Solar System

    • How it works: Directly connected to the utility power grid, using the grid as a virtual battery.
    • Pros: Most affordable, uses net metering to send excess power back, reducing bills.
    • Cons: Does not provide power during blackouts.
    • Best for: Urban and suburban homes with grid access.

    Off-Grid Solar System

    • How it works: Completely disconnected from the utility grid, relying on large solar arrays and battery banks for storage.
    • Pros: Total energy independence, ideal for remote locations.
    • Cons: High initial cost (large batteries), often needs a generator backup.
    • Best for: Remote properties without grid access.

    Hybrid Solar System

    • How it works: A grid-tied system with added battery storage, offering both grid connection and backup power.
    • Pros: Saves money and provides power during outages (blackout protection).
    • Cons: More complex and expensive than basic grid-tied systems.
    • Best for: Homeowners wanting the best of both worlds: savings and security.
